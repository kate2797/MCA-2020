Describing advantages of a time-frequency analysis over a waveform-based analysis

The representation of sound we get from a waveform-based analysis gives us a clear sense of how loud or quiet a song is at any point in time. It is easy to detect silences as well as the loudest parts of a song that tends to denote the chorus. It is also possible to get a sense of the overall emotion associated with a particular song by looking at a waveform. For example, by looking at the waveform of Indust which is a techno piece, we can clearly see that the song is very loud and energetic without the need to listen to it. Overall, this representation is closer to how humans perceive frequencies. On the other hand, there is little information about what frequencies are present. Frequency changes can be seen on a spectrogram representation. Since each note in music is associated with a certain frequency, it is also possible to get an idea of which note is being played at a given time. Besides frequency information, spectrograms also visualise amplitude (loudness). For example, if we look at the red-coloured parts on any of spectrograms shown above, we can easily detect the loudest parts of a piece.
