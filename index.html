Week 1

Task 1: Dataset theme

My dataset will be centred around Arctic Monkeys, an award-winning English (indie) rock band. Arctic Monkeys was a clear choice for the theme of my dataset as I am their huge fan and I believe that this project will allow me to explore their work on a deeper level. In addition, I managed to find all types of data required for this project, namely, descriptive, notated and acoustic data very easily.


The band was formed in 2002 in Sheffield by Alex Turner (signer, songwriter) and guitarist Jamie Cook. Other members include Nick O'Malley (bass guitar, backing vocals) and Matt Helders (drums, backing vocals). Up to now, Arctic Monkeys released 6 albums, with their debut album, Whatever People Say I Am, That's What I'm Not (2006), being the fastest-selling debut album by a band in UK chart history. What's also interesting is the fact that Arctic Monkeys is seen as one of the first bands to gain public recognition via the Internet, gaining popularity through MySpace and peer-to-peer file sharing services.

Task 2: Current manifestations of data relating to my dataset

Interestingly, the band's official website does not feature any information about the band's biography or discography. Therefore, for someone unfamiliar with Arctic Monkeys, it might be quite difficult to quickly situate them in the music landscape. The official website, however, provides links to all major music streaming platforms (e.g. Spotify, Apple Music, Deezer, YouTube Music or TuneIn) where one can discover more information. I chose to explore how their music is curated on Spotify, Apple Music and Deezer more closely.
